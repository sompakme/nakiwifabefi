    About Brass Roots

    Brass Roots is a New Orleans-based snacks brand that offers a range of products made with Sacha Inchi Seeds. These snacks are keto-friendly, vegan, plant-based, and low FODMAP. Sacha Inchi Seeds are known for their high protein content, fiber, and omega-3 fatty acids.

    The company’s product lineup includes Crunch Puffs, roasted Sacha Inchi Seeds, and butter. Brass Roots partners with farms in Thailand and Laos to source their specialty seeds.

    These Sacha Inchi Seed snacks provide a healthy alternative to traditional snacks, allowing consumers to indulge guilt-free. Whether you are looking for a savory or crunchy snack, Brass Roots has a variety of options to satisfy your cravings while providing essential nutrients.

    The Founder of Brass Roots

    Aaron Gailmor is the visionary behind Brass Roots, serving as the founder and CEO of the company. With a passion for nutrition and a desire to make a positive impact, Gailmor established Brass Roots in 2019.

    Prior to starting Brass Roots, Gailmor had a successful career in various positions at Citibank. However, his entrepreneurial spirit led him to co-found the SuperEats company in 2013, where he gained valuable experience in the health food industry.

    Gailmor’s personal journey with nutrition began when he conducted research to find solutions for his father’s heart condition. Inspired by the power of healthy eating, he set out to create a brand that would provide delicious and nutritious snack alternatives.

    Brass Roots is the result of Gailmor’s determination and passion for promoting a healthier lifestyle. With his background in finance and experience in the health food industry, he brings a unique perspective to the company’s growth and success.

    Brass Roots’ Shark Tank Pitch

    During his Shark Tank pitch, Aaron Gailmor presented his compelling proposal for Brass Roots, seeking a $400,000 investment in exchange for a 7% equity stake. This opportunity allowed Gailmor to introduce the Sharks to the remarkable potential of Sacha Inchi Seeds and highlight the impressive financial progress of his company.

    Brass Roots had experienced significant sales growth, capturing the attention of investors. However, it was important to note that despite this success, the company had yet to achieve profitability. The Sharks expressed concerns surrounding various aspects such as the product’s pricing, profit margins, and the competitive landscape of the market.

    “Although Brass Roots has demonstrated impressive sales growth, we have reservations about entering this highly competitive market. The pricing and profit margins will be challenging to navigate.”

    Ultimately, none of the Sharks made an investment in Brass Roots. However, Gailmor’s pitch provided invaluable exposure for the company and reinforced its commitment to offer innovative, healthy snack alternatives.
