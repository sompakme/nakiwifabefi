    Gary Woodland is an American professional golfer who plays on the PGA Tour.

    Born May 21, 1984, in Topeka, Kansas, Woodland is the son of Dan and Linda Woodland.

    He completed Shawnee Heights High School in the suburb of Tecumseh and proceeded to Washburn University in Topeka on a basketball scholarship, but left after his freshman year to attend the University of Kansas in Lawrence on a golf scholarship.

    Woodland earned a degree in sociology from the University of Kansas, where he played golf.

    Following a successful college career, he turned pro in 2007 and briefly competed on the circuit then known as the Nationwide Tour, now the Korn Ferry Tour.

    Woodland won the U.S. Open in 2019, his first major championship and sixth professional victory.

    He has competed on the PGA Tour since 2009 and has four wins.

    Woodland has been married to Interior Designer Gabby Granado since 2016.

    Jaxson Lynn Woodland is the firstborn child of Gary Woodland and Gabby Granado.

    His parents were reportedly pregnant with twins. However, due to some unknown circumstances, they have to endure one of the baby’s losses.

    Jaxson happens to be the other twin that survived. He was born on June 26, 2017.

    He has two sisters who are also twins; Maddox and Lennox Woodland.

    Jaxson Lynn Woodland is 5 years old as of 2022.
