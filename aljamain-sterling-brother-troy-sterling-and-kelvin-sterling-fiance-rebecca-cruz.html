    Aljamain Sterling, a Jamaican-American mixed martial artist, has two brothers named Troy and Kevin Sterling. All three brothers have pursued careers in mixed martial arts.

    Aljamain is the current UFC Bantamweight Champion and participates in the Ultimate Fighting Championship’s (UFC) Bantamweight division. He also participated in the Cage Fury Fighting Championship, where he previously held the title of bantamweight. He is #7 in the UFC men’s pound-for-pound rankings as of August 23, 2022.

    Sterling won by submission to debut his amateur career in 2009.; he had a 6-1 amateur record before going pro, with his only loss coming through a split decision, which he would later get revenge on. Along the journey, Sterling won the Extreme FC amateur featherweight championship and the Raging Wolf amateur bantamweight championship.

    The three brothers are making their name in the fighting industry, where Troy and Kevin have been motivated by their brother Aljamain.

    The Highlight Of Aljamain Sterling Career: UFC Bantamweight Champion

    Aljamain has had a great career as a fighter, but the peak of his achievements must be the UFC Bantamweight championship. He ranks among the best lightweight fighters, and his belt proves this statement.

    Sterling was scheduled to compete against Petr Yan for the UFC Bantamweight Championship at UFC 256 on December 12, 2020. However, the fight was dropped from the UFC 256 program for unclear reasons on November 22 and took place at UFC 259 on March 6, 2021.

    Sterling won the fight by disqualification due to an intentional, illegal knee in the chest and announced the new UFC Bantamweight Champion in the fourth round. He made history in the UFC, becoming the first fighter to achieve a victory through disqualification.

    It was anticipated that Yan would face him again at UFC 267 on October 30, 2021. Sterling, however, withdrew from the competition on September 25 due to ongoing neck problems brought on by surgery. The fight was initially scheduled to occur on March 5, 2022, at UFC 272, but was moved to UFC 273 in April.

    Sterling won in a tough contest and successfully defended his title via a split decision. Five of the 18 MMA media outlets gave the fight a draw, while the other 11 gave Sterling the victory.
