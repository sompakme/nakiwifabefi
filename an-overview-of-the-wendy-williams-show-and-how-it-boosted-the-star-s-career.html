    Wendy Williams didn't sing or act, but she definitely knew how to relate to her audience by providing brutally honest, and sometimes highly controversial commentary on pop culture. In fact, the more she stirred the pot, the more interested her audience became, and in no time at all, she was reaching millions of viewers with each episode. She had already seen early success as a radio talk show host and her name started to seep through mainstream audiences during those days, but when she locked in to host The Wendy Williams Show, she really proved to be a force to be reckoned with, and her income began to dramatically increase.

    Unapologetically Wendy

    The biggest factor to Wendy's success is her ability to be unapologetically true to herself. She isn't afraid to speak her mind, and most often fails to sugarcoat topics or prepare audiences for the sharp-tongued rants that she launches. She has managed to connect with her audience by being authentic to her roots and true to herself, often time talking to fans about her personal health struggles, and sharing information with them about her failed marriage. Wendy's honest recounts of her own life experiences make it easier for fans to resonate with her on a personal level, and fans have loved the experience of not knowing what will come next after Williams stars talking.

    The Wendy Williams Show has provided Williams with a platform to convey her personal thoughts and feelings, and simultaneously gave her an opening to lash out against celebrities that she didn't feel fondly towards. The network was seeing huge profits, and Wendy saw her personal wealth increase dramatically.

    Massive Financial Gains

    When Wendy Williams first entered the entertainment world, she was earning as little as $3.75 per hour for her time a a radio broadcaster with Virgin Islands. After investing several years into her radio hosting career she was offered the opportunity to step out from behind the microphone and make the move to being in front of the cameras while appearing on television.

    After entering the world of television and throwing herself into The Wendy Williams Show, she saw a dramatic increase in her fanbase, as well huge gains within her bank account, as well.

    Entering The Multi-Millionaires Club

    With her career now firmly in place, Williams' fame and fortune were both dramatically elevated. The once-modest radio host was now embraced by her fans each time she crossed the stage, and her fortune was rapidly growing. Thanks to her huge success as the host of the Wendy Williams Show, Wendy's fortune was growing dramatically. At the height of her career, Williams had amassed and astronomical $60 million net worth.

    In spite of her ailing health and her failure to retain her tenure on the show, Wendy Williams has managed to hang on to a $20 million fortune, which is still more than she will ever need to live in the lap of luxury and enjoy her life to the fullest.

    Keeping Current Amid Constant Controversy

    For the majority of her career, Wendy Williams refused to engage on social media. In fact, he doesn't even email. In 2016 she openly declared to fans that she "doesn't do" email or messaging and that she will resist them at every opportunity.  This is notably significant considering that her rise to fame and the acquisition of her fortune came during the depths of the digital age. This was a time that the majority of celebrities and citizens alike couldn't fathom making an impact on the world without the use of their social media apps. The Wendy Williams Show became the singular platform on which Wendy conveyed her messaging to fans, and still, she managed to reach incredible heights of fame while she steadily grew her net worth.

    Much of this is credited to the level of controversy that Wendy became known for. She was known to make off-beat and unpopular statements, and has even gone so far as to make brazen allegations surrounding the sexual orientation of huge celebrities, inclusive of the legendary Whitney Houston. She has heartlessly dismissed the death of TikTok star, Swavy , and has ruthlessly made fun of the cleft lip scar of Joaquin Phoenix, and that's just to name a few of her most heated moments. There were several. Fans can't seem to look away from the chaos. Her controversial statements drummed up high ratings for the show and kept increasing Wendy's fame and fortune.

    Thanks to her antics, Williams was earning a whopping $55,-000 per episode, which equated to a jaw dropping $10 million a year.

    Wendy Gets A Star On The Hollywood Walk Of Fame

    Thanks to the enormity of her success on the Wendy Williams Show, Wendy managed to clinch the highly coveted honor of having her very own star on the legendary Hollywood Walk Of Fame. The Hollywood Chamber of Commerce awarded her the honor of claiming the 2677th star on October 17th, 2019. She was now officially part of the most iconic footprint of the entertainment industry, forever immortalizing her success in the Category of Television.

    Endorsement Deals And Expansion

    With the ratings of the Wendy Williams Show soaring high, Wendy's income was monstrous, and the clever entrepreneur monetized on her fame even further by pursuing other avenues and generating additional income streams. She began to take on lucrative endorsement deals, earning millions by lending her celebrity status to well known brands. She also went on to become a New York Times Best Seller for Ask Wendy: Straight Up Life Advice for All the Drama In Your Life.
