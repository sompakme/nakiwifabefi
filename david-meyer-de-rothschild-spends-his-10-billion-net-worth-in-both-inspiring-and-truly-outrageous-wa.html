    8 David Mayer De Rothschild Is A Well Known Author

    According to Style, David Mayer de Rothschild has significantly contributed to the environmental sustainability literature. In 2007, he authored “The Live Earth Global Warming Survival Handbook: 77 Essential Skills to Stop Climate Change” - “Or Live Through It”, a comprehensive guide offering actionable steps to mitigate climate change.

    Venturing into children's literature in 2010, he wrote a graphic novel, “The Boy, The Girl & The Tree”, aimed at inspiring younger audiences to care for the environment. His 2011 publication, ”The Plastiki: An Adventure to Save Our Oceans”, shares his journey across the Pacific Ocean on a vessel built from reclaimed plastic bottles.

    7 David Mayer De Rothschild Invests In Boats Made From Plastic Bottles

    According to Oceanplasticleanup, David Mayer de Rothschild embarked on a unique Pacific journey in 2012. The voyage was conducted on a special 60-foot catamaran named the Plastiki, built exclusively from recycled material known as srPET. Developed in Europe, srPET is similar in strength to fiberglass but is made from 100% recycled plastic. The Plastiki's twin hulls were molded from 12,000 recycled two-litre plastic bottles.

    The Plastiki's name is a homage to the Kon-Tiki, a balsa wood raft that completed a 7000-kilometer Pacific journey in 1947. David Mayer de Rothschild's voyage took three-and-a-half months, starting from San Francisco and concluding in Sydney. Also, the voyage's primary focus was the Great Pacific Garbage Patch.

    However, the Plastiki's journey also brought attention to several environmental issues, including the sinking islands of Tuvalu, mass pollution, underwater nuclear testing, and coral bleaching. David Mayer de Rothschild highlighted that the problem was not with the plastic itself but with the lack of proper understanding of its use and reuse.
